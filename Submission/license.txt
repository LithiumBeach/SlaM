\b \cf2 Inclusiveness Policy
\b0 \
The Global Game Jam\'ae (GGJ) focuses on bringing communities together through collaboration, innovation and experimentation and our website engages that spirit. No sexism, racism, discrimination or any kind of exclusion will be tolerated in association with the GGJ and we reserve the right to refuse and/or remove content based on our principles. We encourage inclusive and welcoming environments in all our locations. We encourage a free and open activity where creativity should know no censorship and we hope all participants will exercise their creative freedoms. We take pride in our jammers and organizers, but the Global Game Jam\'a0Inc. is not liable for anyone's specific views and opinions.\

\b \cf2 Content Policy
\b0 \
The Global Game Jam\'ae is about experimentation, sharing, collaboration and learning from each other. All Jammers are expected to share their games by uploading their games, assets, source code etc.\
Games are uploaded under the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/4.0/"}}{\fldrslt \cf3 \ul \ulc3 \strokec3 Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike 4.0 Creative Commons license}} ({\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/4.0/"}}{\fldrslt \cf3 \ul \ulc3 \strokec3 https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/4.0/}}).\
This basically means the team that created the game, owns the original content and can do with it as they please, and that Global Game Jam\'ae retains an original copy that other people can download, modify and distribute for non-commercial purposes.\'a0\
All files necessary to run the game, as well as all assets, source\'a0code and project files created during the GGJ and necessary to\'a0rebuild/compile & link the game from scratch must be uploaded as well.\'a0Third party tools, libraries, engines and assets that are used for the\'a0game, but not created during the GGJ, may be omitted from the GGJ\'a0project submission. However, clear instructions must be included on\'a0how to purchase or obtain these resources in order to continue\'a0development on the game.\
GGJ also reserves the right to remove any uploaded games if the content is offensive or unsuitable. This\'a0\uc0\u8236 includes but is not limited to:\u8237  games with\'a0\u8236 harmful\u8237  \u8236 or\u8237  \u8236 prejudicial\u8237  themes\'a0related\u8237  \u8236 to\u8237  \u8236 gender,\u8237  \u8236 sexual\u8237  \u8236 orientation,\u8237  age, \u8236 ability,\u8237  \u8236 appearance, ethnicity,\u8237  national origin, \u8236 citizenship, socioeconomic\u8237  \u8236 status,\u8237  \u8236 or\u8237  \u8236 religion\u8237 . \b Policy for Email Usage (general)
\b0 \
Global Game Jam\'ae respects our community member\'92s privacy. As a non-profit organization, Global Game Jam\'ae has never sold or otherwise shared participant email for the purpose of profit to any third party organizations. \'a0As of 2015 the organization is distinguishing our contact information into two categories. \'a0Our general email list will remain a private list, used only for official communication from the the Global Game Jam non profit and will include the GGJ Newsletter. Our new, opt-in lists provides an opportunity for members of our Global Game Jam community network to contact Global Game Jam participants. These opt-in-lists contain only the email addresses of individuals who have explicitly indicated interest in GGJ announcements from sponsors and promotions.\
Consent to this general email usage policy is granted by participating in or registering for the Global Game Jam\'ae.\

\b Global Game Jam\'ae Policy on becoming a jam site
\b0 \
One of the things that make the GGJ unique, is that we require all jammers to participate from an approved location. A jam site will typically, but not always, be open to anyone who wants to participate. Most GGJ sites tend to be companies, co-working spaces or\'a0universities.\
We discourage use of private residences and\'a0very\'a0rarely approve them.\'a0\
It is not possible to participate in GGJ as a jammer without being associated to a physical approved site. This means we do not accept virtual sites or remote participants as a default.\
All GGJ sites must, at\'a0minimum, provide free Internet access and space to set up and work comfortably.\

\b GGJ Research
\b0 \
GGJ conducts studies and research related to each global Game Jam event.\'a0GGJ research strives to explore, understand and grow the art of games and game jamming. We endeavor to protect the rights, welfare, and well-being of subjects involved in research conducted or supported by Global Game Jam\'ae and the various researchers by adhering to strict legal and ethical protocols. Research is primarily conducted via survey, or observation. GGJ participants may choose to opt-out of interviews and observations by advising their local organizers or by not taking the survey.\
\'a0\

\b Age Policy
\b0 \
No person may participate in a Global Game Jam event\'ae unless they have reached 18 years of age or the age of majority (e.g. are legal adults) in the country of participation (whichever is younger). As an example, participants attending a GGJ site in Scotland or Cuba, must be 16 years of age or older to participate as that is the age of majority, while participants that join a site in the United States, New Zealand or Japan must be 18+ to attend.\
The only exception to this rule, is if the minor is accompanied by a legal guardian at all times while attending the jam.\
